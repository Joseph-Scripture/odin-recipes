French Toast

Description

A timeless breakfast classic, this French toast recipe creates a dish that is wonderfully custardy on the inside with a crisp, golden-brown exterior. The secret is using thick-sliced, slightly stale bread which soaks up the rich egg mixture without becoming soggy. It's a simple yet elegant meal perfect for a lazy weekend brunch.

Ingredients

  • 8 slices thick-cut bread
  • 4 large eggs
  • A cup of milk
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla
  • 2 teaspoon granulated cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • Butter
  • Marple syrup

Steps

  1. Make the Custard: In a shallow dish (like a pie plate), whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, cinnamon, sugar, and salt until well combined and frothy.
  2. Soak the Bread: Dip each slice of bread into the egg mixture, allowing it to soak for about 20-30 seconds on each side. The bread should be fully saturated but not falling apart.
  3. Cook the French Toast: Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat and melt a tablespoon of butter. Place the soaked bread slices on the skillet, being careful not to overcrowd it. Cook for 2-3 minutes per side, until golden brown and crisp.
  4. Serve: Serve immediately, topped with a pat of butter, a drizzle of warm maple syrup, and a dusting of powdered sugar if desired.
